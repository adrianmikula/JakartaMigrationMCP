## 2. Sole Trader vs Company: The Perception Reality

### Legally

You can sell software as a sole trader. That is not the issue.

### Perception-wise

Enterprise buyers care about **risk**, not structure.

They ask:

* Who maintains this?
* What happens if it breaks?
* Can we audit it?
* Can we stop using it safely?

They do **not** ask:

* Is this a Pty Ltd / LLC?
* How many employees do you have?

### Practical Recommendation

**Short term (now):**

* Continue operating as a sole trader
* Trade as either:

  * `<Your Name> Software`, or
  * `<Product Name> by <Your Name>`

**Medium term (after revenue):**

* Form a company only when:

  * Contracts require it, or
  * You want liability separation

Do **not** block monetisation waiting for incorporation.

### 4. Deterministic, Reversible Behaviour

Enterprise teams trust tools that:

* Produce diffs
* Can be run in dry-run mode

* Produce audit logs

Even simple claims like:

* “Never commits automatically”
* “Produces full migration report”

Your buyers are risk managers, not startup founders.
